HISTORY

Hyperadrenocorticism 'HAC' was first discovered not in animals but in humans by American neurosurgeon Harvey Cushing sometime in the early 1900's and was subsequently named after him when he published a paper on the condition some years later in 1932.

Interestingly around the middle of the century it was found that there was also a paper published in 1924 by a lesser known Russian neuropathologist N.M. Itsenko outlining the same basic symptoms and disorder and is now known as Itsenko-Cushings in Russia.

'HAC' is a progressive disease which occurs when the adrenal glands produce an excess of the steroid hormone Cortisol which is harmful to the liver and pancreas functions in too large and too little amounts (Too little known as Addison's disease).

While recordeed cases of 'HAC' in humans are relatively rare, cases in certain dog breeds* especially around middle age are much more common although sometimes due to difficulties in accurately diagnosing the condition can lead to it being attributed to possible other ailments.
