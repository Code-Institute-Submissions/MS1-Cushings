DIAGNOSIS

Tyson was taken to the University hospital for small animals and put through a series of tests and scans to check for any growths or tumors either on the Pituitary gland or Adrenal glands.
Problems with the Pituitary gland account for approximatley 80% of all cases of cushings and is located at the bottom of the brain. It's function is to send messages to the adrenal glands near the kidneys to produce the stress hormone Cortisol, if either of these glands develop a tumor or growth this can massively increase the amount of Cortisol produced which can lead to damage of the Liver and Pancreas.
Tyson had to have his belly shaved for these scans and the hair in this area has yet to grow back some 7 months later. (As seen in main image)
It can be a very difficult condition to diagnose as it can show as various other ailments that many middle age dogs can suffer from. Therefore it can be a bit of trial and error with a selection of blood tests, urine tests, hormone screening tests and scans.

LDDS TEST

Low dose dexamethasone suppression is a test where a sample of blood
is taken before a synthetic version of Cortisol is administered into the
system after a required period of time another sample is taken and the
compared to asses any differences in the levels.

ACTH TEST

Adrenocorticotropic hormone is a hormone produced by the pituitary gland
to regulate the production of Cortisol. Once again blood samples are taken
before and after a dose is administered to asses any differences caused.
